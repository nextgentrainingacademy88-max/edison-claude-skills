8 UGC SCRIPT FORMATS FOR AI UGC ADS
======================================
Use these formats when building ad script concepts. DR (Format 1) must always be one of your selected formats.

AI GENERATION NOTES:
- All 8 formats below are confirmed viable for AI video generation.
- Formats 6 (GRWM) and 7 (Day-in-the-Life) from the original library have been removed — they require continuous product application sequences and 5–10+ location changes with consistent identity, which AI video cannot reliably produce.
- B-roll instructions in all formats must follow AI B-roll rules (see Script_Pacing_Guide.md).

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FORMAT 1: FAST 5-PART DIRECT RESPONSE (DR)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Structure: Hook → Problem → Solution → Proof/Social Proof → CTA
Best for: Cold traffic, scalable testing, any awareness stage
Why it works: Compresses a complete sales conversation into a mini-story. Stops scroll, validates pain, presents fix, removes doubt, drives action. Highest conversion reliability across all testing.
AI suitability: STRONG — mostly talking head with simple product B-roll inserts. The format AI was built for.

Typical beats:
- Hook (0–3s): Call out pain/outcome with relatable question, bold statement, or visual
- Problem (3–8s): Make it personal, use VOC language to validate the pain
- Solution (8–16s): Introduce product naturally — show it, name it, explain how it helps
- Proof (16–25s): Product beauty shot or creator with product (medium shot)
- CTA (last 3–5s): One clear action

FORMAT 2: SCENARIO-BASED SKIT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Structure: Hook (relatable scene) → Agitate struggle → Solution reveal → Value/Proof → CTA
Best for: Unaware, Problem Aware audiences.
Why it works: Dramatizes everyday frustrations, sells the problem first, builds emotional connection before reveal.
AI suitability: MODERATE — single character in 1–2 simple environments. No prop interaction. No fast movement. Keep each scene to 5–10 seconds.
AI constraint: One character only. Two environments maximum. No physical comedic actions or prop handling. Energy comes from delivery and pacing, not movement.

Typical beats:
- Hook (0–3s): Set the relatable scene/situation — creator already in the environment
- Agitate (3–10s): Amplify the pain through talking, expression, and delivery
- Reveal (10–18s): Natural pivot to the product (shown in medium shot or as product B-roll)
- Value (18–28s): Quick win + specifics
- CTA (last 3–5s): Simple, direct

FORMAT 3: PROBLEM-AGITATE-SOLUTION (PAS)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Structure: Problem → Agitate (intensify pain) → Solution
Best for: Pain-point products (skincare, cleaning, productivity). Problem Aware, Solution Aware.
Why it works: Digs deeper into frustration before offering relief. Makes "before" feel unbearable, then delivers the easy fix.
AI suitability: STRONG — talking head throughout, product B-roll for solution proof. Clean two-part structure AI handles well.

Typical beats:
- Problem (0–6s): Present the recognizable issue
- Agitate (6–18s): Amplify the cost — what happens if you don't solve it
- Solution (18–35s): Introduce product as talking head + product beauty shot B-roll
- CTA (last 3–5s): Urgent close

FORMAT 4: DISCOVERY STORY / TRANSFORMATION NARRATIVE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Structure: Hook → Relatable "before" (verbal) → Discovery moment → Proof/specifics → CTA
Best for: Beauty, wellness, fitness, productivity. Solution Aware, Product Aware.
Why it works: Turns the ad into a short emotional journey. Reduces resistance for skeptical buyers.
AI suitability: MODERATE — works as a verbal narrative. The "before" and "after" are described in speech, not shown as literal visual transformation. Visual proof is product beauty shots and lifestyle compositions.
AI constraint: Do NOT script literal before/after visual evidence of a person's appearance changing. Script the transformation as a first-person spoken narrative. A before/after split, if desired, is a post-production composite of two separately generated clips.

Typical beats:
- Hook (0–3s): Tease the transformation or the pain (verbally + strong visual hook)
- Before (3–8s): Creator describes the frustration in their own words (talking head)
- Discovery (8–14s): Natural find — how they came across the product
- Proof (14–30s): Specific results described verbally + product beauty shot B-roll
- CTA (last 3–5s): "See the difference yourself"

FORMAT 5: EXPERT COMMENTARY / NATIVE TESTIMONIAL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Structure: Hook (authority line) → Expert insight/experience → Proof → CTA
Best for: Complex products, supplements, tech, retargeting. Solution Aware, Product Aware.
Why it works: Mimics podcast clip or real user voice for instant credibility. Educates before pitching.
AI suitability: STRONG — single talking head with authority delivery. AI avatar tools handle expert tone well. Specify "confident, measured, knowledgeable delivery" in emotional direction.

Typical beats:
- Hook (0–3s): Strongest authority line or provocative question
- Expert insight (3–15s): Share knowledge, experience, or credentials (talking head)
- Proof (15–30s): Stats, product close-up, specific result described verbally
- CTA (last 3–5s): "Try the better way"

FORMAT 6: FOUNDER / ORIGIN STORY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Structure: Hook → Why I created this → The problem I saw → How it works → Proof → CTA
Best for: Founder-led brands, small DTC, differentiated products. Problem Aware, Solution Aware.
Why it works: Founder stories build trust through authenticity and purpose. The "why" is often more compelling than the "what."
AI suitability: STRONG — single talking head, personal story. One character, consistent identity. Specify the same character reference across all clips.

Typical beats:
- Hook (0–3s): "I created [product] because..." or provocative industry insight
- Origin (3–10s): The personal frustration or insight that led to the product
- Problem (10–16s): Why existing solutions failed
- Solution (16–25s): What makes this different (mechanism) + product beauty shot B-roll
- Proof (25–35s): Results, reviews mentioned verbally
- CTA (last 3–5s): Direct, personal

FORMAT 7: LISTICLE / "TOP X REASONS"
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Structure: Hook (number promise) → Reason 1 → Reason 2 → Reason 3 → [Optional: Reason 4–5] → CTA
Best for: Feature-rich products, products needing education. Solution Aware, Product Aware.
Why it works: Numbers in hooks drive curiosity. Each reason is a mini-hook that keeps retention. Feels educational, not salesy.
AI suitability: STRONG — talking head with B-roll inserts per point. Clean format that AI handles well.
AI note: Number graphics and "Reason 1 / Reason 2" text callouts between points must be added in post-production — not generated in-clip.

Typical beats:
- Hook (0–3s): "X reasons [product] is different" or "X things I wish I knew about [category]"
- Reason 1 (3–10s): Strongest benefit — lead with the best (talking head + product B-roll)
- Reason 2 (10–18s): Second benefit (talking head + product B-roll)
- Reason 3 (18–25s): Third benefit — can be emotional/social proof
- CTA (last 3–5s): "Link in bio" or trial/offer

FORMAT 8: REACTION / RESPONSE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Structure: Hook (react to something) → Response/opinion → Proof/experience → CTA
Best for: Contrarian angles, belief-flip, trend-riding. Problem Aware, Solution Aware.
Why it works: Reaction content is native to TikTok/Reels. The creator's personality drives trust.
AI suitability: MODERATE — the reaction itself (talking head with strong emotional delivery) is fully AI-generatable. The green-screen composite is post-production.
AI note: Script the creator's reaction as a standalone talking-head clip. The "reacted-to" content (article, comment, stat) is specified separately as a post-production element. Strong facial expression and vocal delivery carry this format without needing the composite in the AI generation phase.

Typical beats:
- Hook (0–3s): Creator reacts visibly to something (expression, delivery) — composite added in post
- Response (3–12s): Share their informed take — agree, disagree, add nuance (talking head)
- Proof (12–25s): Back it up with personal experience + product B-roll
- CTA (last 3–5s): Natural, educational

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
REMOVED FORMATS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

GRWM (GET READY WITH ME) — REMOVED
Reason: Requires continuous product application sequences across multiple steps — hands applying makeup, skincare, hair products. Combines every major AI failure mode: complex hand-product interaction, physics simulation, and identity consistency across many clips in different stages of a routine. Not viable for fully AI-generated production.

DAY-IN-THE-LIFE — REMOVED
Reason: Requires 5–10+ distinct location and time-of-day scenes with perfect identity consistency, varied activities (many involving object interaction), and a natural, continuous feeling. Cumulative identity drift and error rate across that many scenes makes a convincing result implausible without extensive manual intervention.
