VISUAL HOOK LIBRARY — AI UGC EDITION
======================================
What the viewer SEES in the first 0–3 seconds. Every hook needs BOTH a verbal hook AND a visual hook.
The visual hook is the pattern interrupt that works even with sound off.

AI GENERATION RULES:
- All hooks in this library are confirmed AI-generatable.
- Removed hooks that require hands doing complex things, mirror reflections, or physical actions AI cannot reliably produce.
- POST-PROD hooks are achievable but must be assembled in editing — not generated as a single AI clip.
- Creator can hold a product casually in a medium or wide shot. Avoid close-up hand focus or complex hand-product interactions.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
AI-GENERATABLE HOOKS (script these freely)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. CLOSE-UP PULL-BACK
Description: Camera starts extremely close to the creator's face or product (filling the frame), then pulls back to normal distance.
Why it works: The extreme close-up is unusual and creates instant curiosity. The pull-back reveals context.
AI note: Camera movement is one of AI video's strongest capabilities. Specify "dolly out from extreme close-up" — reliably executed.
Best for: Review hooks, confession hooks, "honest take" hooks

2. PRODUCT SLIDE REVEAL
Description: Product glides or rotates into frame from the side or bottom, or camera pushes toward a product already resting on a surface.
Why it works: Creates "what is that?" curiosity. The product IS the visual hook.
AI note: Product-only reveals with no hands are reliable. Do NOT script a hand carrying or placing the product. Product moves, or camera moves — not a hand.
Best for: Discovery hooks, "must-have" hooks, product intro hooks

3. LOW ANGLE / LEAN IN
Description: Camera is at a low angle (table level or below), creator leans in from above. Conspiratorial, intimate energy.
Why it works: Unusual angle = automatic attention. Creates an "I'm about to tell you something" feeling.
AI note: Low angle compositions are achievable. Script as "creator already in frame at low angle" — do not script the action of setting a phone down.
Best for: Confession hooks, "don't judge me" hooks, secret/tip hooks

4. SLOW APPROACH
Description: Creator moves slowly toward camera while talking. Gentle, controlled forward motion.
Why it works: Movement in the first frame triggers attention. A calm approach creates intimacy and pull.
AI note: Slow, controlled movement maintains identity consistency. Do NOT script fast walking, bursting in, or quick movement — AI degrades with fast motion. Specify "slow approach" or "gentle movement toward camera."
Best for: Energy hooks, lifestyle hooks, "let me show you" hooks

5. HOLD-UP PRODUCT (medium shot)
Description: Creator holds the product up toward camera in a relaxed, natural way. Medium shot — product is visible but hands are not the focus.
Why it works: Answers "what is this about?" instantly. Works for Product Aware and Most Aware.
AI note: Casual product holding in a medium or wide shot is achievable. Do NOT script close-up hand focus, product manipulation, or the creator picking up/putting down the product. The creator and product are already in frame together.
Best for: Review hooks, comparison hooks, "worth it?" hooks

6. WHISPER / LEAN-IN
Description: Creator leans close to camera, lowers voice, creates ASMR-adjacent intimacy.
Why it works: Contrast with normal content volume. Creates "secret" feeling. Viewer leans in mentally.
AI note: Simple forward head movement with emotional delivery cue is fully reliable. Specify "whispered delivery" or "leaning close, hushed tone."
Best for: Secret/tip hooks, confession hooks, "nobody talks about this" hooks

7. DRAMATIC PAUSE / STARE
Description: Creator holds eye contact with the camera for 1–2 seconds before speaking. Slight head tilt or raised eyebrow optional.
Why it works: Silence in a feed full of noise is jarring. Creates "what's about to happen?" tension.
AI note: Near-static talking head is the most reliable AI generation possible. Zero artifact risk.
Best for: Belief flip hooks, dramatic revelation hooks, "nobody talks about this" hooks

8. PRODUCT BEAUTY SHOT (opening B-roll)
Description: First shot is the product itself — rotating slowly on a surface, in a lifestyle setting, lit beautifully.
Why it works: Leads with visual proof of the product. Works for Product Aware and Most Aware audiences.
AI note: Product beauty shots are AI video's most commercially mature output. Specify composition, surface, lighting mood. No hands.
Best for: Product aware hooks, review hooks, "worth it?" hooks

9. FLAT LAY REVEAL
Description: Overhead camera angle, products arranged aesthetically on a surface. Camera descends slowly or zooms out to reveal the full arrangement.
Why it works: Clean, visual, product-focused. Works especially well for beauty, wellness, and lifestyle.
AI note: Top-down compositions without hands are reliably generated. Do NOT script hands placing items. Products are already arranged — camera reveals them.
Best for: Haul hooks, "essentials" hooks, routine hooks, multi-product brands

10. OVER-THE-SHOULDER / POV
Description: Camera shows the creator's perspective — looking at a product on a surface, looking at a phone screen, looking at a scene.
Why it works: Puts the viewer IN the creator's shoes. First-person perspective = immediate immersion.
AI note: POV compositions are achievable. Do NOT script visible hands reaching toward or interacting with objects in POV. The view is observational.
Best for: POV hooks, "look at this" hooks, discovery hooks

11. PRODUCT LIFESTYLE SHOT (opening B-roll)
Description: Product shown in a natural lifestyle environment — on a bathroom counter, next to a coffee cup, on a workout bench — with no hands.
Why it works: Shows the product in context without feeling like an ad. Invites the viewer into the lifestyle.
AI note: Lifestyle compositions without hands are fully reliable. Specify environment, lighting, and mood.
Best for: Unaware hooks, lifestyle hooks, aspirational hooks

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
POST-PRODUCTION HOOKS (not AI-generated — assembled in editing)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

These hooks require combining multiple clips or adding overlay elements in post. Script them as instructions for the editing step, not as AI generation prompts.

POST-1. BEFORE/AFTER SPLIT
What it is: Screen split showing "before" on one side and "after" on the other.
How to build it: Generate two separate talking-head or product clips. Composite in CapCut, Premiere, or similar.
Best for: Transformation hooks, review/timeframe hooks

POST-2. GREEN SCREEN REACTION
What it is: Creator composited in front of an article, comment, stat, or product page.
How to build it: Generate the talking-head reaction clip on a clean background. Add the background content layer in editing using background removal + compositor.
Best for: Reaction hooks, belief flip hooks, "let's fact-check" hooks

POST-3. TEXT ON SCREEN + TALKING
What it is: Bold text overlay appears on screen while creator talks.
How to build it: Generate the talking-head clip. Add text overlays, captions, and graphic elements in post-production. AI-generated text in video is unreliable — always add in editing.
Best for: Stat hooks, mistake hooks, bold claim hooks
Note: All captions, CTAs, pricing, and product names shown on screen must be added in post. Never rely on AI to render readable text.

POST-4. SCREEN RECORDING + FACE BUBBLE
What it is: Screen recording of an app/website/results with the creator's face in a small bubble.
How to build it: Generate the face-cam talking head separately. Composite as picture-in-picture in editing.
Best for: Results hooks, demo hooks, "let me show you" hooks

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
REMOVED HOOKS (not viable for AI generation)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The following hooks have been removed because AI video cannot reliably generate them:

FINGER TAP / SCREEN TAP — Fine finger articulation toward a lens is a known AI failure point.
UNBOXING MOMENT — Hands opening packaging combines three simultaneous AI failure modes.
MIRROR SHOT — Mirror reflections require 3D spatial reasoning current AI architectures cannot perform.
HAND GESTURE / COUNTING — Specific finger positions are not reliably generatable.
CHAOTIC ENERGY / BURST IN — Fast movement universally degrades AI video quality.
PRODUCT IN USE close-up — Complex hand-product application is not scriptable. Use PRODUCT BEAUTY SHOT instead.
COMPARISON SIDE-BY-SIDE hand-held — Use FLAT LAY REVEAL for comparisons instead.
