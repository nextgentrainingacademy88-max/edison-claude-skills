UGC Script Layout — AI UGC Edition (Premium Template)
=======================================================

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HOW LINES SHOULD SOUND (READ THIS FIRST)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Every spoken line in every script that comes out of this skill gets voiced by an AI
avatar. The voice will deliver EXACTLY what you write. Write a written sentence and
you get a robotic read. Write a spoken line and you get a natural UGC ad.

This is the single most important thing about this file. See
`Natural_Speech_Rules.md` for the full 12 rules — but the short version is below.

THE SOUND YOU'RE GOING FOR
A confident, articulate friend talking candidly to their phone about a product they
actually like. Not a presenter. Not a news anchor. Not a narrator. A friend.

CORE MOVES THAT MAKE A LINE SOUND SPOKEN
- Contractions everywhere: I'm, you're, it's, don't, can't, haven't, I've, you'll
- Varied line length — using complete clauses of different lengths (short complete thoughts mixed with long complete thoughts). NOT by tacking fragments onto sentence ends.
- Short complete thoughts are fine when they stand alone: "This stuff actually works." / "I'm not even exaggerating."
- BANNED: cutoff-stinger fragments appended to an already-complete sentence ("...died on Meta. Two seconds." / "...for four days. Four."). This is the #1 failure mode of this skill. Zero exceptions.
- Mid-thought openers (1–2 per script max): "So —" / "Honestly," / "Okay so —" / "Look,"
- Strategic filler (2–3 per script total, not per line): like, kind of, literally, honestly, I mean
- One idea per line — no adjective stacks
- Concrete over abstract: "I slept through the night for the first time in months" beats "I slept better"

BANNED WORDS (if any appear in a line, rewrite the line from scratch)
game-changer, revolutionary, unlock, elevate, transform your life, introducing, discover,
premium, state-of-the-art, cutting-edge, seamlessly, optimal results, harness,
scientifically formulated, moreover, furthermore, additionally

THE LINE RHYTHM TEST
After writing a line, ask yourself:
1. Would I text this to my best friend about a product I liked? If no, rewrite.
2. Does it have a contraction where it could? If no, add one.
3. Is it one clean thought, or three stacked ideas? If stacked, split or cut.
4. Read it mentally — does it sound like speech, or like text? If text, rewrite.

Every example in this file below has already been written to pass these tests.
Use the rhythm, the contractions, and the complete-clause structure as your template.

CORE RULE (AI UGC reality)
AI UGC is a single AI actor talking to camera, while visuals change between scenes:
- Main Footage: creator talking to camera (sync audio — the talking-head clip)
- B-roll: visuals change while the creator keeps talking as voiceover, OR it's silent + music only
Every scene must make it obvious which type of footage is happening.

AI GENERATION RULES FOR EVERY SCENE:
1. Creator can hold the product casually in a medium or wide shot. Never script close-up hand focus or complex hand-product interaction.
2. All B-roll must come from the AI-reliable B-roll list (see below).
3. Never script readable text inside an AI video clip. All text callouts, captions, and CTAs are added in post-production.
4. Avoid scripting fast movement. Slow, controlled motion only.
5. One character only per script. No multi-person scenes.

AI-RELIABLE B-ROLL TYPES (script these freely):
✅ Product rotating or floating on a clean surface — studio lighting, no hands
✅ Product on a lifestyle surface — bathroom counter, desk, kitchen, gym — no hands
✅ Product texture / ingredient close-up — skin, powder, liquid in still container, fabric
✅ Overhead flat lay of product(s) on a surface — camera descends or zooms
✅ Slow push-in toward a product on a surface
✅ Nature / environment establishing shot (forest, cityscape, sunlight through window)
✅ Abstract mood shot — light flare, bokeh, soft atmosphere
✅ Product beauty shot from different angles — orbit, close-up, wide

B-ROLL TYPES TO AVOID (AI generates these unreliably):
❌ Liquid pouring, splashing, or flowing
❌ Hands opening, squeezing, or applying a product
❌ Fabric or clothing in dynamic motion
❌ Food preparation or cooking
❌ Any shot requiring readable text on packaging or screen
❌ Fast movement or action sequences

SCENE STRUCTURE — use this exactly for every scene
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Scene #X
🗣 Talking point (word-for-word)
(Emotion + speaking style in parentheses)
🎥 Main Footage: [what we see — creator composition, angle, framing]
(AUDIO: sync talking to camera)
🎬 B-roll: [what we see] (optional — only from AI-reliable B-roll list)
🔊 Background voice during B-roll (required if B-roll exists):
Choose ONE:
   Voiceover (word-for-word): creator continues speaking over b-roll
   OR
   No voiceover (music only): visuals only, no speech

CTA Rule: CTA must be the final scene's talking point. Creator talks directly to camera.
