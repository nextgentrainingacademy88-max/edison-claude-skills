NATURAL SPEECH RULES — THE MOST IMPORTANT FILE IN THIS SKILL
=============================================================
Read this before writing a single line of script. Read it again after writing every draft.

THE ONE RULE
Every line you write gets spoken out loud by an AI avatar. If a line doesn't sound like something
a real person would actually say to a friend on camera, the ad dies on arrival. Robot lines
produce robot voice delivery. Robot voice delivery gets scrolled past in 0.5 seconds.

The job of this file is to make sure you never hand off a script that sounds written.
You are not writing ad copy. You are writing speech. Speech is different from writing.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE 12 RULES OF NATURAL UGC SPEECH
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RULE 1 — CONTRACTIONS, ALWAYS
Real people use contractions. Writing avoids them. Robots avoid them.
- "I am" → "I'm"
- "You are" → "You're"
- "It is" → "it's"
- "Do not" → "don't"
- "Cannot" → "can't"
- "Will not" → "won't"
- "Have not" → "haven't"
- "I have been" → "I've been"
- "You will" → "you'll"

If a line has a full uncontracted form, you wrote it, you didn't say it. Rewrite.

RULE 2 — WRITE FOR THE EAR, NOT THE EYE
After drafting a line, read it in your head as if an actual human were saying it. Ask:
- Would I actually say this out loud?
- Would I text this to a friend?
- Does the rhythm sound like speech or like a landing page?
If any answer is no — rewrite until yes.

RULE 3 — WRITE COMPLETE THOUGHTS. NO PUNCHY CUTOFF FRAGMENTS.
This is the single most important rule in this entire file. Read it twice.

Real people speaking on camera do NOT tack punchy 1–3 word fragments onto the end of
their sentences. That is a STATIC AD COPYWRITING TRICK. It's what you see in written
Facebook ad copy to create rhythm on the page. It is NOT how humans actually talk.

When a human is talking to a camera, their thoughts come out as complete clauses —
short or long, but complete. They might speak a 4-word thought, or they might speak
a 14-word thought, but they don't speak a 9-word thought and then bolt a 2-word
stinger onto the end of it for emphasis. That is a writer's move. Nobody talks like that.

❌ BANNED PATTERN — THE STATIC-AD CUTOFF STINGER:
- "Nobody told me how fast ad creative dies on Meta. Two seconds."
- "I burned through my entire budget in a week. One week."
- "The results blew me away. Insane."
- "I've been using this for a month. A month."
- "My skin cleared up in four days. Four."
- "That was it. That was the whole thing."

Every one of those is a written ad writer echoing a number or word for dramatic
effect. A real person talking to their phone would say:

✅ NATURAL:
- "Nobody told me how fast ad creative dies on Meta — it's like two seconds and you're cooked."
- "I burned through my entire budget in a week which was kind of insane."
- "The results honestly blew me away, I wasn't expecting that at all."
- "I've been using this for about a month now and it's been good."
- "My skin cleared up in like four days which I still don't fully believe."

Notice what's happening in the naturals: the thought lands as one complete clause,
or it lands and then continues naturally into the next thought. It does NOT land
and then get a punchy echo stinger slapped onto the end.

The only fragments that are allowed are ones that stand entirely on their own as a
sentence — not ones appended to an already-complete sentence for impact. Example:
- "Okay so here's the thing." ← allowed (it's a complete spoken thought)
- "I was done. Completely done." ← BANNED (echo stinger)

When in doubt: if you can delete the fragment and the previous sentence still works,
you're looking at a banned echo stinger. Delete it or fold it in naturally.

RULE 4 — VARY LINE LENGTH WITH COMPLETE CLAUSES
Don't make every line the same length. But vary them with full thoughts, not fragment stingers:
- A short complete line: "I actually can't believe this works."
- A medium complete line: "I tried three different things before this and none of them did anything."
- A long complete line that unspools naturally: "Okay so I've been running Meta ads for like two years now and the one thing that kept killing me was how fast creative burned out."

Each of those is a complete spoken thought. None of them ends on a 2-word echo stinger.
Rhythm comes from varying the LENGTH of complete clauses, not from bolting fragments
onto the ends of sentences. That's the difference between writing for the ear (rhythm
through clause length) and writing for the eye (rhythm through punctuation tricks).

RULE 5 — START LINES IN THE MIDDLE OF A THOUGHT SOMETIMES
Real people don't always begin with a clean opener. They start mid-thought. Use sparingly
(1–2 times per script max, or it gets performative):
- "So — okay, I wasn't gonna tell anyone about this but..."
- "Look, I've been putting this off for months and..."
- "Honestly? I didn't think it would work."
- "Okay so hear me out —"
- "Yeah, I'll just say it —"

RULE 6 — FILLER AND IMPERFECTION (SPARINGLY)
Light, natural filler earns trust. Heavy filler feels fake. Use these at most 2–3 times per script,
not in every line:
- "like" (as in "it was like, insane")
- "kind of"
- "literally" (only when it's emphatic)
- "honestly"
- "I mean"
- "you know"
- "basically"
- Ellipses "..." for the natural pause in speech

Never stack fillers. "Like, honestly, I mean, basically..." is not natural — it's a parody.
One filler per line, max. And most lines should have zero.

RULE 7 — USE EMOTIONS FROM VOC, NOT LITERAL VOC KEYWORDS
This is the single biggest keyword-stuffing mistake.

WRONG: VOC says customers are "frustrated with wasted ad spend and inconsistent creative."
You write: "If you're frustrated with wasted ad spend and inconsistent creative, watch this."
Result: A line with all the right keywords that sounds like nobody has ever said.

RIGHT: VOC tells you customers FEEL stuck, embarrassed by their ad results, sick of
burning money on stuff that doesn't work. You write:
"If you're still throwing money at ads that don't hit — yeah, same. I had to stop."

You're matching the FEELING, not the dictionary words. Pull the emotion out of VOC and
write a line that would sound natural coming from someone who feels that way.

RULE 8 — BAN MARKETING WORDS AND PHRASES
These are instant robot tells. If any appear in a line, rewrite that line from scratch:
- "game-changer" / "game-changing"
- "revolutionary" / "cutting-edge" / "state-of-the-art"
- "unlike anything else"
- "premium quality" / "premium formula"
- "harness the power of"
- "unlock"
- "elevate your [anything]"
- "transform your life"
- "seamlessly integrates"
- "scientifically formulated" / "expertly crafted"
- "optimal results"
- "introducing"
- "discover"

Also ban marketing transition words that no human says in conversation:
- "Moreover" / "Furthermore" / "Additionally" → say "And", "Plus", "Oh and", or start a new sentence.

RULE 9 — CONCRETE AND SPECIFIC BEATS ABSTRACT EVERY TIME
Abstract = writerly = robotic. Concrete = human.
- ABSTRACT: "I started sleeping better."
- CONCRETE: "I actually slept through the night. For the first time in months."
- ABSTRACT: "My skin improved."
- CONCRETE: "The little dry patch on my cheek that's been there all year? Gone."
- ABSTRACT: "It boosted my energy."
- CONCRETE: "I didn't crash at 2pm for the first time in I don't know how long."

Specificity makes a claim feel true. Abstraction makes it feel written.

RULE 10 — DON'T FRONTLOAD THE CLAIM LIKE A PRESS RELEASE
Written ads put the benefit up front. Human speech buries the lede and circles around.
- WRITTEN: "This serum reduces wrinkles by 40% in two weeks."
- SPOKEN: "Okay so I've been using this for — what, like two weeks? And the lines around my eyes? They're basically not there anymore. I don't even know how that's possible."

The second one sells harder because it sounds real.

RULE 11 — ONE THOUGHT PER LINE (NO ADJECTIVE STACKING)
- ROBOT: "This premium, fast-absorbing, dermatologist-tested serum delivers visible radiance."
- HUMAN: "This stuff sinks in in like ten seconds. That's the whole thing I love about it."

Stop stacking adjectives. One idea at a time. That's how people talk.

RULE 12 — THE TEXT-A-FRIEND TEST
Before finalizing any line, ask: "Would I text this to my best friend about a product I liked?"
If the answer is no, rewrite.

You text a friend like: "dude this serum is insane, my skin is glowing for no reason"
You don't text a friend like: "Experience radiant, luminous skin with this dermatologist-approved formula"

Every line in every script in this skill should pass the text-a-friend test.

THE #1 FAILURE MODE — THE STATIC-AD CUTOFF STINGER
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This is the single failure mode that has killed more UGC scripts from this skill than
any other issue. You will see it try to appear in almost every draft you generate.
You must hunt it down and kill it every time.

THE TRAP: After writing a normal sentence, your instinct is to add a short echo fragment
at the end for "punch" or "rhythm." This is what written static ad copy does — because
static ad copy has to create rhythm on the page through punctuation and line breaks.
UGC scripts get SPOKEN, so the rhythm comes from the actual clause lengths and the
performer's delivery. The fragment stinger is not only unnecessary — it sounds fake
the moment an AI voice says it out loud, because no human talking to their phone
camera actually echoes a word for emphasis like that.

STUDY THESE — every one of these was an actual failure this skill produced:

❌ FAILED: "Nobody told me how fast ad creative dies on Meta. Two seconds."
✅ FIXED:  "Nobody told me how fast ad creative dies on Meta — like, two seconds and you're done."

❌ FAILED: "I spent forever trying AI ad tools that kept spitting out the same stuff. Every single one."
✅ FIXED:  "I spent forever trying AI ad tools and every single one of them kept spitting out the same stuff."

❌ FAILED: "My pipeline is full every week now. Every week."
✅ FIXED:  "My pipeline is full every week now, which still feels weird to say."

❌ FAILED: "The thing nobody tells you about Meta? Creative volume."
✅ FIXED:  "The thing nobody tells you about Meta is that it all comes down to creative volume."

❌ FAILED: "I was close to hiring a designer every week. Every single week."
✅ FIXED:  "I was honestly this close to just hiring a designer every single week."

THE TEST:
For every line you write, ask: is there a short fragment tacked onto the end of a
sentence that's already complete? If yes — delete the fragment or fold it into the
main clause. No exceptions. Not even once per script. Zero.

Even this classic VOC-research-looking move:
❌ "I don't even know how much money I burned on stuff that did nothing. Thousands."
✅ "I don't even know how much money I burned on stuff that didn't work, probably thousands at this point."

THE SAME MOVE APPLIES TO NUMBERS:
Numbers are the #1 thing that tempts a fragment stinger. Resist every single time.
- ❌ "I noticed a difference in two weeks. Two."
- ✅ "I noticed a difference in like two weeks which I wasn't expecting."
- ❌ "It took four days. Four days."
- ✅ "It took four days which, you know, is basically nothing."

THE HOOK PATTERN TRANSLATION MOVE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The Hook Library gives you PATTERNS. Patterns are SHAPES, not fill-in-the-blank templates.

Pattern: "If you're still dealing with [pain], watch this"
❌ FILL-IN-THE-BLANK (robot): "If you're still dealing with inconsistent ad creative output and wasted budget, watch this"
✅ TRANSLATED (human): "If you're still paying people to make bad ads — stop. Seriously, watch this."

The shape is preserved ("if you're still [doing bad thing], [call to watch]"). The language
is rewritten to sound spoken, not stuffed.

Pattern: "The #1 mistake people make with [X]"
❌ FILL-IN-THE-BLANK: "The #1 mistake people make with Meta ad creative generation workflows"
✅ TRANSLATED: "The biggest thing people get wrong about ad creative? They think it's a skill problem. It's not."

Pattern: "I thought [common belief] until I saw this"
❌ FILL-IN-THE-BLANK: "I thought premium DTC supplements were all overpriced marketing until I saw this"
✅ TRANSLATED: "I used to think every supplement brand was basically the same marketing scam. Yeah, until I saw this."

THE MANDATORY REWRITE PASS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

After you draft every scene's dialogue, do this pass BEFORE outputting:

1. READ IT (mentally, as speech) — Does it sound like speech or like text?
2. CONTRACTION CHECK — Any uncontracted forms? Contract them.
3. BUZZWORD CHECK — Any banned marketing words from Rule 8? Rewrite the line.
4. ABSTRACT CHECK — Any vague benefit claims? Make them concrete.
5. RHYTHM CHECK — Are the lines all similar length? Vary them — using complete clauses of different lengths, NOT by tacking fragments onto the ends.
6. **STINGER CHECK (critical)** — Does any line end with a short fragment tacked onto a sentence that's already complete? If yes — delete the fragment or fold it naturally into the main clause. This is the #1 failure mode of this skill. Hunt it every single time.
7. FRIEND TEST — Would you text this to your best friend? If no, rewrite.
8. EMOTIONAL AUTHENTICITY — Does the line sound like it came from a feeling, or from a keyword list? If keyword list, rewrite.

Every line must pass all 8. This pass is not optional. This is the difference between
a script that sells and a script that gets ignored.

WHAT THIS FILE IS NOT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This file is not telling you to make scripts unprofessional, sloppy, or unclear.
Natural ≠ sloppy. Natural means: it sounds like a real, articulate person talking
candidly to the camera about a product they like. Think of a smart, confident friend
who happens to be good on camera. Not a news anchor. Not a press release. Not an AI.

Every script this skill produces should sound like it was spoken, not written.
That is the entire point of UGC — and the entire point of this file.
