DTC CATEGORY ANGLE PLAYBOOK
=============================
Which angle types tend to work best for each major DTC product category. Use as guidance for angle selection — not hard rules.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SKINCARE / BEAUTY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Top-performing angle types:
1. Before and After (#4) — Skincare is visual. Transformation photos/video are the strongest proof.
2. Review Ads (#16) — "After 30/60/90 days" honest reviews build trust in a skeptical category.
3. Ingredient Spotlight (#24) — Educated skincare consumers want to know WHY it works (active ingredients, concentrations, formulation).
4. Challenge Beliefs (#11) — "Your routine is the problem, not the products" type contrarian angles cut through noise.
5. Compare to Rival (#5) — Customers constantly compare serums, moisturizers, SPFs. Comparison content is native to the category.

Common VOC themes to build angles around:
- Failed products / wasted money (tried everything, nothing works)
- Skin texture/tone concerns (dull, uneven, textured, aging)
- Ingredient confusion (too many options, don't know what works)
- Routine fatigue (too many steps, too complicated)
- Sensitivity/reaction fear (worried about breakouts, irritation)

Category-specific notes:
- Show real skin texture. Overly filtered content kills trust.
- Time-based proof is expected (day 1 vs. day 30)
- Ingredient education angles work especially well for serums and actives
- "My dermatologist said..." or "As an esthetician..." authority angles are powerful

SUPPLEMENTS / HEALTH
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Top-performing angle types:
1. Challenge Beliefs (#11) — Supplements face extreme skepticism. Contrarian angles ("what you think you know about [X] is wrong") break through.
2. Remove Limitations (#6) — "Even if nothing has worked before" angles address the #1 objection.
3. Speed (#2) — "Noticed a difference in X days" angles with realistic timeframes overcome the "do I have to take this for months?" objection.
4. Creator Authority (#22) — Someone who has "tested everything" lending credibility is powerful.
5. Ingredient Spotlight (#24) — "Most [supplement type] use [cheap form]. This uses [premium form]. Here's why that matters."

Common VOC themes:
- Nothing works / been disappointed too many times
- Don't trust supplement claims / skepticism
- Tired of taking 10 pills a day / simplicity
- Specific health goal (energy, sleep, gut, focus, inflammation)
- Price sensitivity (supplements are expensive for something you can't see)

Category-specific notes:
- COMPLIANCE IS CRITICAL. No medical claims, no diagnosis, no "cures" language.
- Frame everything as personal experience: "I noticed..." not "This will..."
- Skeptic-to-believer arcs are the gold standard script format
- Specific, modest results are more believable than dramatic claims
- Gut health, sleep, and energy are the 3 most emotionally resonant supplement categories

FITNESS / EQUIPMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Top-performing angle types:
1. Compare to Rival (#5) — "Home gym vs. gym membership" or "This vs. [competitor equipment]" is the dominant angle.
2. Remove Limitations (#6) — "No experience needed" / "works in small spaces" / "takes 15 minutes" remove barriers.
3. Before and After (#4) — Body transformation is visual and undeniable proof.
4. Routine Integration (#19) — "Fits into my morning routine" / "I use it while watching TV" angles sell convenience.
5. Features to Benefits (#15) — Fitness customers are feature-aware. Show the feature, then show the real-life benefit.

Common VOC themes:
- Gym intimidation / judgment / social anxiety
- Cost of gym membership vs. home workout
- Space constraints (apartment, small room)
- Boredom with current routine
- Time efficiency (no commute, quick workouts)

Category-specific notes:
- Movement footage is essential — show the product in use
- "Replaced my gym" is the most powerful comparison frame
- Real bodies outperform fitness model bodies in UGC ads
- Space/size demonstrations are critical for home equipment

PET PRODUCTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Top-performing angle types:
1. Call Out Person (#13) — Pet parent identity is extremely strong. "If you're a [breed] parent..." hooks are native.
2. Problem-Aware (#14) — Specific pet issues (allergies, anxiety, digestion) are emotionally charged for pet parents.
3. Review with Results (#16) — "After 2 weeks on this food, my dog's coat..." — time-based results with visible proof.
4. Before and After (#4) — Coat quality, energy level, behavior changes — transformation is powerful.
5. Compare to Rival (#5) — "I switched from [big brand] and the difference is insane" — pet parents love this.

Common VOC themes:
- Pet health anxiety (worried about ingredients, long-term effects)
- Failed products (tried everything for allergies/digestion/anxiety)
- Breed-specific issues (golden retrievers + sensitive stomachs, french bulldogs + skin, etc.)
- Vet recommendations (trust authority)
- Guilt (feeling like they're not giving their pet the best)

Category-specific notes:
- Pet parents respond to their pet's REACTION more than product features
- Breed callouts create instant identity connection
- Visible pet health changes (coat, energy, behavior) are the strongest proof
- Emotional stakes are extremely high — pet parents treat pets like children
- NOTE: Actual pet footage is almost always MUST-FILM — AI struggles with realistic pet behavior

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HOME / KITCHEN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Top-performing angle types:
1. Problem-Aware (#14) — "The real reason your [home thing] doesn't work" — root cause angles.
2. Compare to Rival (#5) — "Old way vs. new way" comparisons are visually compelling for home products.
3. Remove Limitations (#6) — "No tools needed" / "takes 5 minutes" / "anyone can do this" — effort reduction.
4. Features to Benefits (#15) — Home products have specific features that map to specific daily benefits. Show both.
5. Routine Integration (#19) — "This changed my morning/cleaning/cooking routine" — daily life improvement.

Common VOC themes:
- Time wasted on ineffective solutions
- Frustration with cleaning/maintenance
- Space constraints (small kitchen, apartment living)
- Quality disappointment (broke after a month, didn't work as advertised)
- Convenience obsession (one product that does it all)

Category-specific notes:
- DEMONSTRATION is king. Show the product solving the problem in real-time.
- Before/after of SPACES (not just products) is extremely effective
- "Wish I bought this sooner" is the most common high-value VOC pattern
- Satisfying visual moments (clean vs. dirty, organized vs. messy) stop the scroll

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FASHION / APPAREL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Top-performing angle types:
1. Call Out Person (#13) — Identity-based angles ("If you're a size [X] who can never find jeans that fit...")
2. Lifestyle Aspiration (#23) — Fashion sells identity. Show the LIFE the customer wants.
3. Social Proof Stack (#21) — "Sold out 3 times" / "Everyone on TikTok is wearing this" — herd behavior.
4. Compare to Rival (#5) — "Looks like [expensive brand] but costs 1/3 the price" — value comparison.
5. Review with Results (#16) — "Wore it every day for a month — here's how it held up" — durability/quality proof.

Common VOC themes:
- Fit inconsistency (looks different online vs. IRL)
- Quality for the price (tired of fast fashion falling apart)
- Finding the right style for their body type
- Comfort vs. aesthetics tradeoff
- Social validation (wanting compliments, feeling confident)

Category-specific notes:
- Try-on content is essential — people need to see how it LOOKS and MOVES on a real body
- Size inclusivity callouts create strong identity connection
- "I get compliments every time" is top-tier social proof
- "Looks expensive" is one of the most powerful DTC fashion phrases
- Mirror/try-on visual hooks are the highest-performing visual format

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FOOD / BEVERAGE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Top-performing angle types:
1. Compare to Rival (#5) — "I replaced [unhealthy option] with this and I don't miss it" — health comparison.
2. Remove Limitations (#6) — "Healthy AND actually tastes good" — removing the taste/health tradeoff.
3. Routine Integration (#19) — "I add this to my morning smoothie" / "This replaced my afternoon snack" — habit integration.
4. Review with Results (#16) — Taste reaction + time-based health results.
5. Social Proof Stack (#21) — "My whole family loves it" / "Even my picky kids eat it" — household adoption.

Common VOC themes:
- Taste/health tradeoff (healthy things taste bad)
- Convenience (don't have time to cook, need quick options)
- Dietary restrictions (finding something that fits their diet AND tastes good)
- Price per serving comparison to alternatives
- Family/household adoption (does everyone in the house like it?)

Category-specific notes:
- TASTE REACTIONS are the single most powerful content type for food/bev
- Show the product being CONSUMED, not just displayed
- "Even my [picky eater/husband/kids] loved it" is extremely powerful social proof
- Texture descriptions matter: "not chalky," "not gritty," "actually smooth"
- Routine integration is more important than standalone use

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TECH ACCESSORIES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Top-performing angle types:
1. Compare to Rival (#5) — "I've had 3 [competitors] break. This one survived [extreme test]" — durability comparison.
2. Features to Benefits (#15) — Tech buyers are feature-literate. Show spec, then show real benefit.
3. Review with Results (#16) — Long-term use reviews ("6 months later") build trust for accessories.
4. Remove Limitations (#6) — "Works with every [device]" / "No setup needed" / "Wireless" — friction removal.
5. Before and After (#4) — Setup quality improvement (desk setup, audio quality, workflow efficiency).

Common VOC themes:
- Durability / build quality (tired of cheap accessories breaking)
- Compatibility anxiety (will it work with MY device?)
- Setup frustration (too complicated, too many cables)
- Value for money (is the premium worth it vs. the cheap option?)
- Productivity/quality improvement (did it actually make a difference?)

Category-specific notes:
- Durability tests / drop tests are inherently engaging content
- "Desk setup" / "WFH upgrade" angles work across many tech accessories
- Side-by-side quality comparisons (audio, video, screen protection) are visual proof
- Unboxing + setup content addresses compatibility and ease-of-use objections simultaneously
