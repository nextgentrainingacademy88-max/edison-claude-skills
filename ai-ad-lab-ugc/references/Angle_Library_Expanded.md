ANGLE LIBRARY (EXPANDED — 24 ANGLE TYPES)
============================================

⚠️  IMPORTANT — ANGLE STATEMENTS ARE INTERNAL CONCEPTS, NOT SPOKEN LINES  ⚠️
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The "Example angle statement" under each angle type is a STRATEGIC CONCEPT used
internally to guide the script. It is **never** a line to be spoken verbatim in a scene.

Every angle statement in this file is written in pitchy, written-copy form on purpose —
so you can understand the strategic positioning at a glance. But if you drop one of
these lines directly into a script, the AI voice will sound like a robot reading a press
release.

Example:

Angle statement (strategic concept — DO NOT SPEAK):
"This serum reduced my dark spots by 60% in 14 days — I have the photos to prove it"

When this concept becomes a spoken line in a script, it must be rewritten into natural
human speech. Something like:

✅ "So I've been using this for like two weeks. And the dark spots I've had forever?
    Basically gone. I have the photos to prove it and I still don't believe it."

Same angle. Same proof. Totally different sound. The second one is what ships.

**Rule: treat angle statements as internal strategy cards. The actual script lines are
always rewritten from scratch through the naturalization pass (see `Natural_Speech_Rules.md`
and Phase 4.5 in `SKILL.md`).**

ORIGINAL 17 ANGLE TYPES (PROVEN)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Measure the Size of the Claim
Trigger: Desire / Proof
Best awareness stage(s): Solution Aware, Product Aware, Most Aware
Proof needed: Results proof (numbers, screenshots, data, case study)
Example angle statement: "This serum reduced my dark spots by 60% in 14 days — I have the photos to prove it"
Best UGC formats: Before/After, Review with results, Demo

2. Measure the Speed of the Claim
Trigger: Desire / Time-to-result
Best awareness stage(s): Problem Aware, Solution Aware, Product Aware
Proof needed: Time-bound proof (timeline, test window, repeatable process)
Example angle statement: "Visible glow in 5 days — not 3 months like every other vitamin C serum promises"
Best UGC formats: Time-lapse review, Day-by-day journal, Before/After

3. Use an Authority
Trigger: Proof / Credibility
Best awareness stage(s): Solution Aware, Product Aware, Most Aware
Proof needed: Verifiable authority signal (expert endorsement, credentials, lab results, celebrity/influencer use)
Example angle statement: "The supplement my naturopath actually takes herself — not just recommends"
Best UGC formats: Expert Commentary, Creator Authority, Founder Story

4. Before and After
Trigger: Proof / Transformation
Best awareness stage(s): Problem Aware, Solution Aware, Product Aware
Proof needed: Before/after evidence + context (what changed + time window)
Example angle statement: "My skin 30 days ago vs. today — same lighting, no filter, just this one product"
Best UGC formats: Transformation Story, Side-by-Side Demo, Photo Journal

5. Compare the Claim to Its Rival
Trigger: Comparison / Differentiation
Best awareness stage(s): Solution Aware, Product Aware
Proof needed: Fair comparison + proof (side-by-side, tests, "why" explanation)
Example angle statement: "I used [competitor] for 6 months and switched — here's why I'm never going back"
Best UGC formats: Comparison, Switch Story, Side-by-Side Test

6. Remove Limitations from the Claim
Trigger: Objection crusher (skill/time/experience barriers)
Best awareness stage(s): Problem Aware, Solution Aware
Proof needed: Clear steps + beginner-friendly proof/examples
Example angle statement: "You don't need a 10-step routine — this one product does the work of five"
Best UGC formats: Problem/Solution, "Even if you..." Testimonial, Simplification Demo

7. State the Claim as a Question
Trigger: Curiosity / Self-identification
Best awareness stage(s): Unaware, Problem Aware, Solution Aware
Proof needed: A specific, believable answer with supporting proof
Example angle statement: "What if the reason your skin looks dull has nothing to do with your routine?"
Best UGC formats: Curiosity Hook UGC, Educational, Myth-Bust

8. Offer Information in the Claim
Trigger: Education / Process
Best awareness stage(s): Problem Aware, Solution Aware
Proof needed: A framework/process + evidence it works
Example angle statement: "The 3-step nighttime routine that dermatologists use on their own skin"
Best UGC formats: Tips/Listicle, "How I...", Educational Demo

9. Stress the Newness of the Claim
Trigger: Novelty / Fresh mechanism
Best awareness stage(s): Solution Aware, Product Aware
Proof needed: What's new + why it matters + proof
Example angle statement: "This isn't just another vitamin C serum — it uses a stabilized form that doesn't oxidize"
Best UGC formats: Unboxing, First Impression, Product Reveal

10. Stress the Exclusiveness of the Claim
Trigger: Uniqueness / Differentiation
Best awareness stage(s): Solution Aware, Product Aware, Most Aware
Proof needed: A real differentiator + proof it's unique
Example angle statement: "The only protein powder with all 9 essential aminos AND it actually tastes like a milkshake"
Best UGC formats: Demo + Taste Test, Comparison, "Why this one" Explainer

11. Challenge Your Prospect's Beliefs
Trigger: Belief reversal / Contrarian insight
Best awareness stage(s): Problem Aware, Solution Aware
Proof needed: Strong reasoning + proof/examples
Example angle statement: "Your skincare routine isn't the problem — the order you apply products is"
Best UGC formats: Myth-Busting, Contrarian Take, "What nobody tells you"

12. Call Out a Solution or Product They're Currently Using
Trigger: Disruption / "Stop doing this"
Best awareness stage(s): Problem Aware, Solution Aware
Proof needed: Why the current approach fails + show alternative
Example angle statement: "Stop wasting money on retinol that's destroying your moisture barrier"
Best UGC formats: Warning/Alert UGC, Comparison, "I made this mistake"

13. Call Out the Person Directly
Trigger: Identity / Direct callout
Best awareness stage(s): Problem Aware, Solution Aware
Proof needed: Persona match + believable solution path
Example angle statement: "To every woman over 30 who's given up on finding a sunscreen that doesn't leave a white cast"
Best UGC formats: Identity UGC, "Message for...", POV

14. Problem-Aware Ad Angle
Trigger: Pain / Root-cause diagnosis
Best awareness stage(s): Problem Aware
Proof needed: Clear diagnosis + why it happens + proof/examples
Example angle statement: "If your protein powder makes you bloated, it's probably the whey concentrate — not your stomach"
Best UGC formats: Problem Diagnosis, "Here's the real reason", Root Cause Reveal

15. Features to Benefits
Trigger: Mechanism leads to Outcome
Best awareness stage(s): Solution Aware, Product Aware
Proof needed: Show the feature working + show the benefit
Example angle statement: "The double-wall insulation keeps your coffee hot for 12 hours — no more reheating at work"
Best UGC formats: Demo, Feature Walkthrough, "Watch this" Proof

16. Review Ads
Trigger: Proof / Social proof
Best awareness stage(s): Product Aware, Most Aware
Proof needed: Real review + authentic delivery
Example angle statement: "Honest review after 90 days — I've reordered 3 times and here's what I actually think"
Best UGC formats: Honest Review, Time-Based Update, "X months later"

17. POV-Style
Trigger: Situation / Moment-of-pain
Best awareness stage(s): Unaware, Problem Aware
Proof needed: Believability + quick clarity (proof can come later in script)
Example angle statement: "POV: You just caught your reflection on a video call and you barely recognize yourself"
Best UGC formats: POV UGC, Relatable Moment, Skit

18. If-Then
Trigger: Logic / Cause-effect diagnosis
Best awareness stage(s): Problem Aware, Solution Aware
Proof needed: Clear reasoning + examples + mechanism
Example angle statement: "If you're bloated after every meal, then it's probably not what you're eating — it's what's missing from your gut"
Best UGC formats: Problem Diagnosis, Educational, Logical Walkthrough

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
NEW ANGLE TYPES (7 DTC-SPECIFIC ADDITIONS)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

19. Routine Integration
Trigger: Convenience / Lifestyle fit
Best awareness stage(s): Solution Aware, Product Aware
Proof needed: Show the product fitting seamlessly into daily routine + outcome
Example angle statement: "I added one step to my morning routine and my skin hasn't looked this good since my twenties"
Best UGC formats: Get Ready With Me (GRWM), Day-in-the-Life, Morning/Night Routine
Why it works for DTC: DTC customers don't want another complicated product — they want something that FITS. This angle says "this is easy and it works within what you already do."

20. Unboxing Discovery
Trigger: Novelty / First impression / Curiosity
Best awareness stage(s): Product Aware, Most Aware
Proof needed: Genuine first reaction + product quality visible
Example angle statement: "I just opened this and I already know this is different — look at this packaging, the texture, the smell"
Best UGC formats: Unboxing, First Impression, "Just arrived" Reveal
Why it works for DTC: The unboxing moment is inherently shareable. First impressions are powerful social proof — especially when the reaction is genuine.

21. Social Proof Stack
Trigger: Herd behavior / Validation / FOMO
Best awareness stage(s): Solution Aware, Product Aware, Most Aware
Proof needed: Multiple proof points stacked (sold out X times, X reviews, featured in X, X people use it)
Example angle statement: "47,000 five-star reviews, sold out 6 times, and my entire friend group uses it — this isn't hype"
Best UGC formats: "Everyone's using this", Trend/Viral, Review Roundup
Why it works for DTC: Stacking multiple social proof signals creates a "this must be real" feeling that overcomes skepticism faster than any single proof point.

22. Creator Authority
Trigger: Personal expertise / Earned credibility
Best awareness stage(s): Problem Aware, Solution Aware, Product Aware
Proof needed: Creator's relevant experience or qualification (not just "I tried it" — more like "I've tested 50 serums in 2 years")
Example angle statement: "I've tested 200+ skincare products on this channel — this is the only vitamin C serum I've ever repurchased"
Best UGC formats: Expert Review, "I've tested everything", Channel/Page Authority
Why it works for DTC: Borrows credibility from someone who has experience depth. Different from "Use an Authority" (#3) because the authority IS the creator, not an external figure.

23. Lifestyle Aspiration
Trigger: Identity / Desired self / Social belonging
Best awareness stage(s): Unaware, Problem Aware, Solution Aware
Proof needed: Visual proof of the aspirational lifestyle + product's role in it
Example angle statement: "This is what my mornings look like now — calm, intentional, and actually good for my skin"
Best UGC formats: Day-in-the-Life, Aesthetic Routine, "This is my life now"
Why it works for DTC: People don't just buy products — they buy identities. This angle sells the LIFE the customer wants, with the product naturally embedded.

24. Ingredient/Mechanism Spotlight
Trigger: Education / Science / Transparency
Best awareness stage(s): Solution Aware, Product Aware
Proof needed: Specific ingredient/mechanism + why it works + visible/felt result
Example angle statement: "Most vitamin C serums use L-ascorbic acid which oxidizes in days — this uses THD ascorbate which stays stable for months"
Best UGC formats: Educational, Ingredient Breakdown, "The science behind", Demo
Why it works for DTC: Educated DTC consumers (especially in skincare, supplements, and health) want to know WHY something works. This angle turns the mechanism into the selling point.
