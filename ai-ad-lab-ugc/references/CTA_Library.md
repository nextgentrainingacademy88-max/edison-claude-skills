PLATFORM-SPECIFIC CTA NOTES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TikTok:
- "Link in bio" is standard and expected
- Keep CTAs under 5 seconds
- Natural, casual delivery (not "salesy")
- "Comment [word] and I'll send you the link" works for engagement

Meta (Feed):
- "Link in bio" or "Click the link" both work
- Can be slightly more direct since viewers expect ads
- Shop Now buttons handle the heavy lifting — verbal CTA just needs to motivate the click

Meta (Reels/Stories):
- "Swipe up" (Stories) or "Link in bio" (Reels)
- Quick and natural
- Can use "tap the link" for Stories

YouTube Shorts:
- "Link in the description" or "Check the link below"
- Slightly more detail is acceptable (YouTube viewers expect substance)
- Can mention website URL directly

CTA DELIVERY RULES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
- CTA is ALWAYS the final scene
- Keep it under 5 seconds of speech
- Deliver with confidence, not desperation
- Match the emotion of the script (if the script is calm, CTA is calm; if the script is excited, CTA matches)
- Never say "Buy now" or "Purchase today" — that's landing page language, not UGC language
- Never use fake urgency ("only 3 left!") unless it's genuinely true
- The CTA should feel like a helpful suggestion, not a demand
