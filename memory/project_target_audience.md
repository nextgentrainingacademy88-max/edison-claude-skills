**Core target audience (in priority order):**

1. **Business owners / founders / solopreneurs** (PRIMARY, 80%+ of posts) — people who
   run their own business and want to use AI tools to grow, save time, or cut costs.
   Pain points: doing too much themselves, needing to replace expensive hires with AI,
   scaling without hiring, finding the tools that actually work.

2. **Business operators doing multiple jobs** (SECONDARY) — the one person in an SME
   who handles accounting + HR + admin + marketing + ops all at once. They want AI to
   do one of those jobs for them.

3. **AI-curious professionals who want to monetize** — people considering starting an
   agency, side-hustle, or consulting practice using AI tools.

**DO NOT address:**
- Trainers, corporate trainers, training providers — Edison IS a trainer, but he doesn't
  talk TO other trainers as the audience. That's insider-talking-to-insiders.
- HR managers, L&D professionals (as audience — they can be mentioned as the PERSON an
  owner is trying to replace/augment with AI, but never written to directly).
- Employers / hiring managers — business owners are the audience, and they're hiring
  their own employees, so "employers" language feels impersonal.

**How this changes the copy voice:**

- ❌ OLD: "As a trainer, you know how hard it is to..."
- ✅ NEW: "As a business owner, you know how hard it is to..."
- ❌ OLD: "Great for HR managers and L&D teams."
- ✅ NEW: "Great for founders juggling HR, admin, and marketing alone."
- ❌ OLD: "Perfect for your corporate training sessions."
- ✅ NEW: "Perfect for running your business leaner without hiring more staff."
- ❌ OLD: "Teach your team AI with..."
- ✅ NEW: "Use AI to do the work instead of hiring another staff..."

**Framing examples for any topic:**
- "How to replace a junior copywriter with Claude" (not "How to train your staff on Claude")
- "Use NotebookLM instead of hiring a research assistant"
- "Run your accounting with ChatGPT — here's how"
- "The one AI tool every business owner should install this week"
- "How I use Claude to do 3 jobs as a solopreneur"

**What stays unchanged:**
- Edison's own role description stays: "HRDC-certified AI educator and corporate trainer
  who also runs a funnel agency". That's his credential/background. The audience he
  writes FOR is business owners.
- Edison can reference training workshops he runs (e.g. "Last week I ran an AI workshop
  for a company with 40 staff") — but only as proof/authority, not as audience signal.

**How to apply in every run:**
- Never open with "Hey trainers" / "As a trainer" / "If you run corporate training".
- Never use hashtags like #CorporateTraining or #LearningAndDevelopment as primary tags.
- CTA framing: "Comment CLAUDE and I'll send the guide" (business owner language) —
  never "Comment if you train teams".
- Focus content on BUSINESS OUTCOMES: save hours, replace hires, cut costs, grow revenue,
  do more alone — not on PEDAGOGY or teaching frameworks.
